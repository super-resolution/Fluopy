TCSPC simulation

So far only suitable for one type of fluorophore (because fluo transitions is an array)
The transition matrix is 0 for excitations. 
While the current time is smaller then the time of the next pulse:
        1. Get the rates (not normalized) of all possible next transitions**
        2. Get the time via the exponential distribution for each rate
        3. Get the next transition and time via the minimum of all times
If the time exceeds the time of the next pulse*, the transition can be put into a waiting array (entry for each fluorophore). If a transition effects multiple fluorophores, it goes to the entry of each of them. The procedure with the waiting array is as follows:
        1. The time is smaller than the smallest waiting time and smaller than the next pulse:
                The transition is executed. If it effects fluorophores that have an entry in the waiting array, these transitions are discarded (even if it effects multiple fluorophores).
        2. The time is larger than the smallest waiting time and the smallest waiting time is smaller than the next pulse time:
                The waiting transition is executed and the new transition is discarded.
        3. The time is larger than the next pulse time and smaller than the smallest waiting time:
                The transition is stored in the waiting array. If it effects fluorophores that have an entry in the waiting array, they are replaced (if  the respective transitions effect multiple fluorophores, the other entries are discarded).
                Go to the next pulse
        4. The time is larger than the smallest waiting time and the smallest waiting time is larger than the next pulse time:
                a. The new transition does not effect any waiting fluorophores:
                        The new transition is stored in the waiting array.
                b. The time is smaller than the waiting time of the effected waiting fluorophores:
                        The new transition is stored in the waiting array  and replaces the respective entries; If the replaced transitions effect multiple fluorophores, the other entries are discarded.
                        Go to the next pulse
                c. Else:
                        Go to the next pulse
*Another chance of excitation. The time is set equal to the time of the pulse.
**The possible transitions are effected by the waiting array. If no entries in the waiting array, all transitions are possible. If there are entries in the waiting array, there will be a laser pulse first. Only those transitions will possible to be drawn, that are associated to the excitation by this pulse. This can be done as long as all energy transfers have S1 as their donor and S1 can only be invoked by the laser pulse (i.e., no FRET to S0 or RISC).

Replaced values in the waiting array are lost; however, if the replacement is replaced, the original transition may be restored (if the new replacing transition does not effect the fluorophore of the original transition). This is relatively unlikely if the time between pulses is long, because replacing transitions are energy transfers, indicating short times until transitions and hence no replacement (but discarding the original transition).  

Why not number of pulses until next excitation if not all S0? If it is higher than sth. in the waiting array, discard and run waiting array?

How to handle energy transfers? An energy transfer always excites the acceptor, hence it changes the photophysical state. This means that technically, the lifetime of the acceptor should be interrupted, i.e., shortened by the energy transfer. This is also the case if the excited acceptor state is being neglected due to a short lifetime and a recycling of the original acceptor state! Hence, an energy transfer transition like S1T1 - S0T2 - S0T1 that is approximated to S1T1 - S0T1 should lead a discarding/replacing of for example ISC stored for T1 in the waiting array.
